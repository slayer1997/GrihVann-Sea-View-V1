Bandra West through the sea-view lens

The view arc

Bandra’s sea-facing stock looks west and south-west across Mahim Bay toward the Bandra–Worli Sea Link. Because the shoreline curves, the best flats capture a wide, wrapping arc rather than a flat horizon: Carter Road and Bandstand towers can see both the open water and the lit Sea Link at night. The trade-off is that the arc is interrupted by headlands, so a ‘sea view’ here is really a bay view — which is exactly why the floor and angle matter.

Obstruction risk

Obstruction risk in Bandra is dominated by the low-rise villa fabric of Pali Hill and the older sea-facing buildings along Carter Road. A mid-floor flat two rows back can lose most of its water to a neighbouring parapet. We report, per unit, which floors clear the front row and where a balcony’s southern edge starts catching the Sea Link — the difference between a full bay view and a sliver.

Orientation & light

West and south-west orientation means strong evening light and dramatic sunsets, but also afternoon heat load on the glass; north-west corners stay cooler. Monsoon-facing units take the full brunt of the south-west winds, so double-glazing and deck depth are worth checking before you fall for the view.
