Sea facing flats in Tardeo

The ridge between Nana Chowk and Altamount Road — height buys you the bay.

Tardeo sits on one of Mumbai’s highest ridges — and in a flat city, elevation is the first ingredient of a sea view.

Tardeo through the sea-view lens

The view arc

Tardeo’s towers rise between Nana Chowk and the Altamount Road ridge. West and south-west facing high floors look over the city to the Arabian Sea and the curve of Haji Ali bay; the very best stack the bay, the Coastal Road and the Worli skyline into one frame. The arc is altitude-dependent — this is a locality where the same building can go from no water to a wide bay view within a handful of floors.

Obstruction risk

Tardeo is dense and still climbing: new supertalls on and around the ridge can annex a neighbour’s view corridor. There is no protected low-rise foreground like a racecourse or a park, so the per-floor, per-stack obstruction report matters more here than almost anywhere else we work. We map exactly which floor the bay comes clear — and what could rise in between.

Orientation & light

West and south-west orientations deliver the sea and the sunset; south-facing high floors trade open water for the harbour glitter of South Mumbai. East-facing units look inland across the city — skyline, not sea.
