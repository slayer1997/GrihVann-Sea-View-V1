Home / Worli Sea Face flats
Worli Sea Face is the yardstick for a sea view in Mumbai — the one address where the flat price is quoted almost entirely by the arc you can see.

Along the Worli Sea Face promenade, apartments look west and south-west over the open Arabian Sea, with the Bandra–Worli Sea Link curving across the frame. Unlike a bay view, this is genuinely open water, and the premium buildings here are engineered around it — which is why price is driven less by carpet area than by floor height and how much of the arc a unit actually holds.

Common questions

As a rule, floors above roughly the 30th get a near-180 degree unobstructed arc from the horizon to the Sea Link. Between the 10th and 30th you clear most of the front row and the Sea Link enters the frame. Below the 10th the view is usually partial and carries the highest obstruction risk.

Indicative public listings put Worli Sea Face around 64,000 to 66,000 rupees per square foot in 2026, with established premium towers a little lower and new branded launches higher. These are researched market ranges, not GrihVann quotes; every GrihVann listing publishes its own developer rate card.

For most buyers on Worli Sea Face the premium holds because the open arc is genuinely scarce and resale demand is deep. The premium is only justified when the specific floor and angle deliver the view; a low or blocked unit sold on a sea-facing label rarely holds it. That is why we publish a per-unit obstruction report.
