Marine Drive is Mumbai’s original sea-facing address — a two-kilometre Art Deco crescent where supply almost never comes up.

Marine Drive through the sea-view lens

The view arc

The Marine Drive arc is the most famous view in the city: a continuous south-to-west sweep of the Arabian Sea tracing the ‘Queen’s Necklace’ of lights from Nariman Point to Malabar Hill. Front-row Deco buildings get an unbroken promenade-and-sea arc; the curve means even mid-line flats keep water in frame. There is no second row that truly counts here — you are either on the necklace or a street behind it.

Obstruction risk

Obstruction is unusual for Mumbai: the promenade is protected, so the sea itself will not be built out. The real constraints are heritage rules (Deco facades limit balcony and window changes) and building age, which caps floor height — so you rarely get a very high vantage, but you almost never lose the view either. We flag which line of buildings sits directly on the arc versus one street back.

Orientation & light

The crescent faces broadly west and south-west, so units run from sunset-facing at the Nariman Point end to softer, side-lit exposures near Walkeshwar. Sea salt and age mean plumbing and wiring in heritage stock deserve scrutiny; the view, however, is as permanent as any in Mumbai.

Is the sea-view premium worth it on Marine Drive?

On Marine Drive the premium buys permanence: the promenade is protected, so the view cannot be built out, and Deco-crescent supply is so thin that front-row units trade privately and hold value across cycles. What you accept in return is heritage constraint and ageing building services, which cap floor height and demand technical due diligence. For a buyer who wants a view that is effectively guaranteed for decades, that trade is often worth it.

Common questions
Will a Marine Drive sea view ever be built out?

No. The promenade is protected, so the sea itself cannot be built over. The real constraints are heritage rules on the Art Deco facades and building age, which cap floor height but also make the view effectively permanent.

What does a sea-facing flat on Marine Drive cost?

Supply is rare and prices vary widely, from roughly 60,000 to over 1,50,000 rupees per square foot depending on building, floor and finish, with 2 BHK tickets often from about 12 crore. These are indicative researched figures.

Which units sit directly on the Marine Drive arc?

Only the front line of Deco buildings truly sits on the crescent; one street back and the continuous sea arc is largely lost. We flag which line a unit is on before anything else.

Are Marine Drive flats a good long-term hold?

For a buyer who wants a view that is effectively guaranteed for decades, the protected promenade and thin supply have kept front-row value resilient across cycles. The trade-off is heritage constraint and ageing building services that need scrutiny.
